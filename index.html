Superstore Project

Global Superstore is a global online retailer based in New York, boasting a broad product catalogue and aiming to be a one-stop-shop for its customers. Global superstore’s clientele, hailing from 147 different countries, can browse through an endless offering with more than 10,000 products. This large selection comprises three main categories: office supplies (e.g., staples), furniture (e.g., chairs), and technology (e.g., smartphones).

In this project, I used Power BI to analyse the 2011 - 2014 sales data.

Malaria Deaths Cases

Malaria is a life-threatening disease caused by Plasmodium parasites that are transmitted to humans through the bites of infected female Anopheles mosquitoes. Despite global efforts to control and eliminate the disease, malaria remains a significant public health challenge, particularly in sub–Saharan Africa, where most malaria cases and deaths occur. Malaria can cause a range of symptoms, including fever, chills, headache, and muscle pain, and if left untreated, can lead to severe complications, including anaemia, respiratory distress, organ failure, and death.

In this project, I used MS-Excel to analyse the deaths due to malaria across different regions, and particular focus was given to Nigeria from 1999 to 2019.

Customer Experience with a Travel Company

In this project, I examined a travel company that is seeking to make customer experience an amazing and unforgettable one. My approach was to gather information about the needs of the customers, their preferences and behaviours through the use of a questionnaire (google form). The information gotten was analysed using my knowledge of data cleaning, pivot-table, and data visualization to gain insights into what the customers want from a travel experience. My findings and recommendations were forwarded to the management of the travel company to enable them design services that are tailored to the needs of their customers.
